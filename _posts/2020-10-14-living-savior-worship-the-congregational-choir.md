LIVING SAVIOR WORSHIP & MUSIC


Have you ever taken the opportunity to look through Scripture and see how many times it talks about “Singing?”  The Psalms are loaded with references to singing.  In fact, there are around ninety instances when the psalmists directs the reader to “sing.”  Psalm 33 uses the word three times in the first three verses. “Sing for joy in the Lord . . .”  “Sing to Him . . .”  “Sing to Him a new song.”  Singing is an act of worship and praise that has been a part of the lives of God’s people throughout history.  (“Then Moses and the Sons of Israel sang this song to the Lord . . .” Ex. 15:1)

The Church throughout her history has had choirs and special ensembles to ensure that there would be singing in its worship.  Over the years, in some situations, the choirs have been thought of as “the singers” to the neglect of the congregation.  The truth, however, is that the congregation is the choir.  Congregational song and praise have been and continue to be at the heart of our corporate worship.

One of my dear friends and mentors, the late Dr. Howie Stevenson, was Minister of Music at The First Evangelical Free Church in Fullerton, CA where Chuck Swindoll served for many years as pastor.  Howie always talked about the importance of the “Congregational Choir.”  He was always encouraging the Body of Christ to sing with enthusiasm and fervor.  In fact, he even coined a little phrase that I borrowed and used in my ministry for many years.  “ALWAYS KEEP ON SINGING!”  Howie was right!  The congregational choir is the most important choir and they need to keep on singing, especially in these days when our ensembles are unable to meet to practice and rehearse.

It is vitally important that as we gather as the Body of Christ, we “Always Keep on Singing!”  It is through our collective voices and praise that we can echo the Song of the Church, “Christ is Risen!”  May our songs of praise always bring honor to the name of Jesus.

Dr. Wallace Horton,
<br>Minister of Worship & Music
